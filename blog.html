Access to Information

What is Access to Information?

Access to information refers to the right to access information held by public bodies or private organizations that perform a public function. The right to access information is essential for transparency and accountability, and it allows citizens to participate in the democratic process. Access to information is also important for individuals, researchers, and businesses to make informed decisions.

Why is Access to Information Important?

Access to information is important for a number of reasons:

  • It promotes transparency and accountability
  • It helps to prevent corruption and abuse of power
  • It allows citizens to participate in the democratic process
  • It promotes informed decision making
  • It allows individuals to protect their rights and interests
